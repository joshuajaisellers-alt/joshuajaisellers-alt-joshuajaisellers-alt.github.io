Transforming the Cleveland County Choral Society
Through Strategic Growth and Digital Engagement

Since 2020, the Cleveland County Choral Society has undergone a remarkable transformation driven by intentional digital strategy, community engagement, and nonprofit development practices. When Joshua Sellers joined the organization as a singing member and volunteer consultant, the choir averaged roughly 30 active singers and operated within modest capacity limits.

Through a comprehensive approach that integrated social media revitalization, website modernization, targeted outreach, and improved organizational infrastructure, the choir experienced unprecedented growth. Membership more than doubled to over 65 singers, prompting a move from First United Methodist Church of Shelby to the larger sanctuary of First Baptist Church Shelby. This expansion has been so significant that the organization is now preparing to relocate rehearsals once again to accommodate its growing ensemble.

The Choral Society’s growth extended beyond membership. Under Joshua’s guidance, the organization strengthened its digital presence, expanded its subscriber base, and secured new grant funding that increased financial stability and artistic capacity. These efforts tripled the budget allocated for instrumentalists, enabling more ambitious programming and higher-quality performances. The combination of strategic communication, nonprofit consulting, and community-centered storytelling not only elevated the choir’s visibility but also deepened its cultural impact within Cleveland County.

Today, the Cleveland County Choral Society stands as a thriving, forward-moving arts organization—an example of how thoughtful digital strategy and mission-aligned leadership can transform a community ensemble into a flourishing regional institution.
